Data Sources and Cleaning


Respiratory Emergencies

Data obtained from Secretaría de Salud at: http://www.dgis.salud.gob.mx/

Year considered: 2016.

Every Dataset contains (among others) the following information for each individual case:
  • Mexican State
  • CLUES: Clave Única de Establecimientos de Salud
  • Sex of the patient
  • Age of the patient
  • Main cause of medical attention


Hospital ID

Clave Única de Establecimientos de Salud (CLUES)
Data obtained from Secretaría de Salud at: http://www.dgis.salud.gob.mx/

Information on each Hospital: Name, address, certification status, etc.


International Clasification of Diseases

Data obtained from World Health Organization at: http://cie10.org/

Catalog of disease codes in order to cross-reference to each patient disease.


Mexico City Air Contaminant Concentrations

Data obtained from Gobierno de la Ciudad de México at: http://www.aire.cdmx.gob.mx/

Year considered: 2016.

Information Obtained:
  • Monitoring Station ID
  • Date
  • Hourly concentrations of:
    • Ozone
    • Particulate Matter 10µm
    • Particulate Matter 2.5µm
    • Carbon Monoxide
    • Nitric Oxide
    • Nitric Dioxide
    • Nitric Oxide Pollutants
    • Sulfur Dioxide


Monitoring Stations Catalog

Data obtained from Gobierno de la Ciudad de México at: http://www.aire.cdmx.gob.mx/

Catalog of Monitoring Stations including information on:
  • Station name and ID
  • Location
  • Contaminants monitored in each station


Parameters Catalog

Data obtained from Gobierno de la Ciudad de México at: http://www.aire.cdmx.gob.mx/

Catalog of Parameters including information on:
  • Contaminant name and ID
  • Unit of Concentration


Units Catalog

Data obtained from Gobierno de la Ciudad de México at: http://www.aire.cdmx.gob.mx/

Catalog of Units including description and ID of every unit.
